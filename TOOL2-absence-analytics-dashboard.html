Manager coaching recommendations — flagged departments

HR strategic principle: Absence data identifies WHERE the problem exists. Manager coaching targets HOW leadership behavior may be contributing. This plan pairs data findings with specific, actionable coaching conversations tailored to each department's context and absence pattern.
Production / Molding
9.4% Q1 avg
Coaching focus areas
  • Shift consistency — review how 2nd and 3rd shift supervisors communicate expectations vs. 1st shift
  • Agency worker integration — are direct employees feeling devalued when agency fills their gaps?
  • Monday pattern analysis — pull day-of-week data to identify chronic Monday callouts
  • Physical environment check — heat, ergonomics, and repetitive motion on molding floor
  • Supervisor availability — are supervisors accessible and supportive or perceived as distant?
Recommended interventions
  • Stay interviews with 2nd and 3rd shift employees specifically
  • Return-to-work structured conversations after each absence event
  • Shift supervisor coaching on recognition and daily communication
Maintenance & Facilities
9.0% Q1 avg · CRITICAL
Coaching focus areas
  • Workload distribution — are mold maintenance techs carrying disproportionate load due to understaffing?
  • Burnout indicators — escalating absence in a small specialized team signals chronic overwork
  • Supervisor responsiveness — does management acknowledge the scarcity challenge openly?
  • Retention risk — each absence in this team raises probability of resignation
Recommended interventions
  • Urgent retention conversation with each mold maintenance tech individually
  • Workload audit — is overtime chronic and unsustainable?
  • Compensation review — are specialized techs compensated at market rate?
  • Cross-training pipeline — begin identifying internal candidates to develop
Shipping & Receiving
8.7% Q1 avg
Coaching focus areas
  • Consistency — absence has been above threshold all three months, suggesting systemic issue not a spike
  • Physical demands — end-of-shift fatigue and physical load in receiving area
  • Manager communication style — are expectations and consequences clearly communicated?
Recommended interventions
  • Attendance policy review and consistent application coaching for supervisor
  • Ergonomic assessment of shipping/receiving floor
  • Pulse survey targeting this department specifically
Quality Control
7.3% Q1 avg · Trending
Coaching focus areas
  • Early warning — January was fine, March hit 8.9% — something changed mid-quarter
  • QC role stress — increased production pressure often flows downstream to QC as rejection rates rise
  • Relationship between QC and Production — tension between departments can drive QC disengagement
Recommended interventions
  • Proactive manager check-in before this becomes a Q2 crisis
  • Review whether production pressure increases in March correlated with QC absence spike
  • Engagement conversation with QC team lead

Root cause analysis — diagnosing the right intervention

HR strategic principle: The data tells you WHERE the problem is. Root cause analysis tells you WHY — and therefore WHAT to do about it. The same absence rate can have completely different causes requiring completely different interventions. This framework prevents HR from applying generic solutions to specific problems.
Production / Molding
Pattern: Escalating
Probable root causes — ranked
  • Shift fatigue — 2nd and 3rd shift physical demands compounded by consecutive scheduling
  • Agency worker dynamic — permanent employees may feel reduced accountability when gaps are always covered
  • Post-holiday burnout — Q1 January start compounds with unresolved December fatigue
  • Ergonomic stress — injection molding floor heat and repetitive motion
  • Supervisor inconsistency across shifts — 1st shift notably lower absence
  • Attendance policy applied unevenly across shifts
Prioritized interventions
  • Stay interviews targeting 2nd and 3rd shift exclusively
  • Agency strategy review — are coverage guarantees reducing absence accountability?
  • Shift supervisor calibration session — align expectations across all three shifts
  • EAP awareness campaign — Q1 is highest stress period for manufacturing workers
  • Ergonomic assessment of molding floor stations
Maintenance & Facilities
Pattern: Escalating · CRITICAL
Probable root causes — ranked
  • Chronic overwork — small specialized team absorbing outsized workload due to hiring scarcity
  • Burnout trajectory — escalating monthly absence in small teams is a resignation precursor
  • Compensation misalignment — mold maintenance techs are market-scarce and may be underpaid
  • Physical toll — mold maintenance is physically demanding and injury-prone
  • Recognition gap — specialized skill often goes unacknowledged by general management
Prioritized interventions
  • Immediate retention conversations — individual meetings with each tech this week
  • Compensation benchmarking — verify market rate for mold maintenance techs in this region
  • Workload audit — document overtime hours over last 90 days
  • Cross-training pipeline — identify and begin developing internal succession candidates
  • Workers comp review — check for unreported or underreported strain injuries
Shipping & Receiving
Pattern: Chronic/Flat
Probable root causes — ranked
  • Policy inconsistency — flat chronic pattern suggests absence is culturally normalized in this dept
  • Supervisor follow-through gap — absence consequences not applied consistently
  • Physical demands — end-of-day load fatigue particularly in receiving
  • Low engagement — absence as passive disengagement signal
Prioritized interventions
  • Attendance policy review and relaunch with consistent enforcement
  • Supervisor coaching on documentation and progressive discipline
  • Engagement pulse survey
  • Return-to-work structured conversation protocol
Quality Control
Pattern: Emerging spike
Probable root causes — ranked
  • Production pressure spillover — March production push likely increased QC rejection pressure
  • Interdepartmental tension — QC/Production friction is a common manufacturing stressor
  • Role stress — QC carries quality accountability without always having authority to slow production
Prioritized interventions
  • Proactive stay interviews before Q2 — catch this before it becomes chronic
  • Review March production targets and QC rejection data for correlation
  • Manager check-in with QC team lead this week

1st shift · 6am–2pm
4.9%
Below threshold — performing well
Headcount: ~100 direct + 20 agency
Pattern: Stable across Q1 — January 4.6%, February 4.8%, March 5.3%
Note: Lower physical fatigue, better supervisor coverage, and daylight hours typically produce lowest absence on 1st shift in manufacturing environments.
Agency use: Minimal — primarily covering planned PTO
2nd shift · 2pm–10pm
7.8%
Approaching threshold — monitor closely
Headcount: ~105 direct + 25 agency
Pattern: Escalating — January 6.9%, February 7.8%, March 8.7%
Note: Mid-afternoon start creates childcare and personal scheduling conflicts. Supervisor bench strength on 2nd shift is thinner — injection molding supervisor vacancy has been open 90+ days.
Agency use: Moderate — covering chronic callouts in molding
3rd shift · 10pm–6am
11.3%
Critical — immediate intervention required
Headcount: ~95 direct + 30 agency
Pattern: Severely escalating — January 9.1%, February 11.0%, March 11.3%
Note: Night shift fatigue, disrupted sleep cycles, and reduced management visibility are primary drivers. Agency dependency is highest on 3rd shift — 30+ agency workers represent approximately 32% of shift headcount.
Agency use: High — agency presence may be reducing accountability culture on this shift
Strategic HR insight — agency dependency risk: The staffing agency supplement has been essential for maintaining production output, but the data suggests a compounding dynamic: as agency presence increases on 2nd and 3rd shifts, absence accountability among direct employees may be declining. When employees know their absence will always be covered, the implicit consequence of calling out is reduced. A structured review of agency utilization strategy is recommended alongside the absence intervention plan.
