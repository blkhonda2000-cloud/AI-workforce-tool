🚥 1. The Risk Tiering System

A simple Red-Yellow-Green framework designed for quick managerial decision-making.

🟢

Low Impact

  • ✔ Drafting standard emails
  • ✔ Brainstorming meeting agendas
  • ✔ Summarizing public articles
Required Action Permitted with standard review.
🟡

High Impact

  • ⚠ Resume & candidate screening
  • ⚠ Performance review drafting
  • ⚠ Compensation modeling
Required Action Requires AI Impact Statement & 100% human-in-the-loop verification.
🔴

Prohibited

  • ✖ Inputting company trade secrets
  • ✖ Processing Social Security Numbers
  • ✖ Non-public financial data analysis
Required Action Strictly forbidden in public tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini).

🔒 2. Data Privacy & Security Guardrails

Protecting organizational assets requires a strict delineation between public experimentation and secure enterprise operations.

🌐

Public vs. Private Models

Employees are absolutely prohibited from entering any proprietary company data into public AI models. Public tools ingest data for training, effectively making private data public.

🏢

Secure Enterprise Environments

Sensitive HR analysis must be conducted only within approved, enterprise-grade systems that maintain strict data isolation (e.g., FedRAMP compliant solutions).

👥 3. Accountability & Transparency

AI is an assistant, not a manager. Final decisions impacting human livelihoods must remain strictly in human hands.

⚖️

The "Human-in-the-Loop" Mandate

AI shall not be used to make final "Life-Cycle" decisions (hiring, firing, or disciplinary actions) without comprehensive, independent human validation.

Mandatory Labeling

"This document was AI-assisted and human-verified."

Any report or document materially created by AI must bear this label to ensure transparency with stakeholders.

🔄 4. Continuous Monitoring

AI models evolve. Our governance cannot remain static. Organizations must perform quarterly Bias and Drift audits to ensure AI outputs remain aligned with company ethics and legal standards.

Q1: Algorithmic Bias Assessment

Reviewing recruiting and screening models for disparate impact across protected demographic classes. Adjusting prompts and weights as necessary.

Q2: Operational Drift Audit

Evaluating if the AI output quality has degraded or shifted away from initial performance baselines.

Q3: Data Privacy Verification

Auditing logs of public AI tools to ensure zero leakage of proprietary data, SSNs, or financials. Reinforcing employee training.

Q4: Comprehensive Framework Review

Updating the Acceptable Use Policy based on new legislation, technological advancements, and internal audit findings from Q1-Q3.
